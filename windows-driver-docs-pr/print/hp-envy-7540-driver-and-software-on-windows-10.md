# HP ENVY e-All-in-One printers on Windows 10


This topic provides troubleshooting tips for HP ENVY e-All-in-One printers and software when upgrading to Windows 10.

## <a href="" id="problem--hp-envy--e-all-in-one-printer-and-software-does-not-migrate-successfully-when-upgrading-to-windows-10"></a>Problem: HP ENVY e-All-in-One printer and software does not migrate successfully when upgrading to Windows 10


After upgrading to Windows 10, you may receive a notification that your HP ENVY e-All-in-One printer has failed to install. To successfully complete the installation, you must remove and reinstall the software and printer after the Windows 10 upgrade as outlined in the procedures below.

### Uninstall the printer driver and software

1.  If your printer is connected to the computer with a USB cable, disconnect the cable from the printer.
2.  Search Windows for *uninstall a program*, and then click the **Change or remove a program** Control panel setting in the list of results.
3.  In the list of installed programs, click your HP printer name, and then click **Uninstall** or **Yes** to confirm the uninstall.
4.  Follow the on-screen instructions to complete the software removal.
5.  Restart the computer.

### Reinstall the printer driver and software

1.  Turn on the printer.
2.  If your printer is connected to the computer with a USB cable, disconnect the cable from the printer.
3.  Go to [HP Customer Support - Software and Driver Downloads](http://go.microsoft.com/fwlink/p/?LinkId=698558).
4.  Select your printer model, and then follow the on-screen instructions to go to the download page.
5.  Click **Download** next to the full feature driver (recommended), or click **Basic Drivers** for other driver options.
6.  Use the **HP Download and Install Assistant** for a guided driver download and installation, or select **Download only** to manually open or run the driver file through your browser’s download tools.

### Restart the computer and the printer

1.  Press the power button on the printer to turn it off.
2.  Close any running programs on your computer, then restart your computer.
3.  Turn on the printer.
4.  Print a document to verify that the printer installation completed successfully.

For more information and a compete guide to troubleshooting your HP ENVY e-All-in-One printer and software using HP's printer diagnostic tools, see [Printer Problems after Upgrading to Windows 10](http://go.microsoft.com/fwlink/p/?LinkId=698312) on the HP support site.
